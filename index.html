Economy of the Baltic States

Welcome to the Baltic States Economy Visualizer using ESRI JavaScript SDK and ChartJS.

The Baltic countries - Estonia, Latvia and Lithuania - share common features and a similar history. These three countries at the EU border with Russia regained their independence from the Soviet Union in the early nineties before joining the EU in 2004 and more recently, the euro area. They display major similarities in terms of their geography, size, economic structure, development and demography; but also some differences, particularly with respect to Estonia, which is often considered ahead of its peers in some ways. GDP per capita

GDP per capita is the sum of gross value added by all resident producers in the economy plus any product taxes (less subsidies) not included in the valuation of output, divided by mid-year population. Growth is calculated from constant price GDP data in local currency. Sustained economic growth increases average incomes and is strongly linked to poverty reduction. GDP per capita provides a basic measure of the value of output per person, which is an indirect indicator of per capita income. Growth in GDP and GDP per capita are considered broad measures of economic growth.
